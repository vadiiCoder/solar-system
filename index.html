Eight worlds — one solar system

01 / Mercury
Mercury
Closest to the Sun

The smallest planet bakes at 430°C by day and plunges to −180°C at night — extreme swings caused by its near-absent atmosphere.

02 / Venus
Venus
Earth's hellish twin

Similar in size to Earth, yet its thick CO₂ atmosphere traps heat and sends surface temperatures soaring past 460°C — hotter than Mercury.

03 / Earth
Earth
The pale blue dot

Our vibrant home. Liquid water, a protective magnetosphere, and the precise tilt that gives us seasons make Earth uniquely alive.

04 / Mars
Mars
The red planet

Iron oxide stains its surface crimson. Home to Olympus Mons — the tallest volcano in the solar system — and humanity's next frontier.

05 / Jupiter
Jupiter
The great giant

More than twice the mass of all other planets combined. Its Great Red Spot — a storm wider than Earth — has raged for centuries.

06 / Saturn
Saturn
Lord of the rings

Its magnificent ring system spans 282,000 km yet is only metres thick. So light it could float on water — density less than 1 g/cm³.

07 / Uranus
Uranus
The tilted world

Rotating on its side at 98°, its poles face the Sun directly. This ice giant's methane-rich atmosphere absorbs red light, giving it a pale teal hue.

08 / Neptune
Neptune
Edge of the known

The most distant known planet, wracked by winds exceeding 2,100 km/h. A single orbit takes 165 Earth years to complete.
